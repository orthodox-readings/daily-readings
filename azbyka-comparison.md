# DailyReadings vs the Slavonic Apps — Comparison for Fr Dionysios

*Prepared at Father's suggestion, comparing our app with azbyka.ru's daily service
texts (the link he sent) and the widely used Russian Orthodox calendar apps.*

---

## What the azbyka "Worships" page is

The link Father sent (azbyka.ru/worships) is azbyka's daily **service-texts**
feature: the complete text of that day's services — Vespers, Matins, Liturgy —
assembled "наряду" (in order, everything already inserted in its place), in
Church Slavonic with Russian guidance. It is built for **readers, choir and
clergy**: it replaces the Menaion, Octoechos and Triodion on the kliros. Azbyka
even notes that singers can serve from printed copies or a tablet. It is a
superb tool — for the people serving the service.

The popular Slavonic calendar apps (azbyka's own «Православный календарь»,
«Календарь Православной Церкви» and similar) sit between that and ours:
daily saints, readings, fasting, plus a built-in prayer book (molitvoslov),
troparia texts, akathists, icons of the saints, and notifications — all in
Slavonic/Russian.

## Why the biggest gap is deliberate

The one large thing the Slavonic apps have that ours does not is **full service
texts**. Two honest reasons not to copy that:

1. **Rights.** The Slavonic and Russian texts are freely reproducible, which is
   why azbyka can assemble them daily. The standard **English** liturgical
   translations (Jordanville, and the texts used across ROCOR parishes) are
   under copyright. We could not lawfully embed them; we would be limited to
   century-old public-domain translations (e.g. Hapgood), which are not what
   the parish serves from.
2. **Audience.** Father's own words — "for laity, yours is already excellent" —
   describe the design goal exactly. A layman preparing for the day needs the
   saints, the readings, the fast and a word from the Fathers; the full kliros
   text belongs on the kliros. Keeping the app small is what makes it get
   opened every morning.

## What to add (proposed)

1. **A daily azbyka link for Slavonic readers.** One tap from the day's page
   straight to azbyka's full service texts for that same day — exactly the page
   Father sent, date-matched automatically. Russian-speaking parishioners get
   the kliros texts; the app stays small. *(Trivial to add.)*
2. **Basic daily prayers card (optional).** A short morning/evening prayers
   section from public-domain translations, kept collapsed so the front page
   stays clean. *(Modest effort; translation choice should have Father's
   blessing.)*
3. **Feast-day notifications.** A gentle reminder on great feasts and the start
   of fasts — best done when/if the app goes to the Google Play store, where
   notifications work properly. *(Later phase.)*

## What to subtract

Nothing. Every current card earns its place, and everything already folds away.
The discipline is in what we continue to leave out: no full service texts, no
akathist library, no icon galleries — the app should remain the layman's
morning companion, not a pocket kliros.
